**Key takeaways on Mosquito Species:**
- While only females blood feed and transmit disease, males occurrence can still tell us that the species is present.
  
- - Aedes aegypti (=Ae aegypti)
Yellow fever mosquito, originated in Africa and has become invasive across the world.
Vector for dengue, chikungunya, Zika, and other anthropogenic viruses.
**CHIKV/CHK (=chikungunya virus)  - transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, especially Ae aegypti.
**DENGUE/DNG/DENG/DENV (=dengue virus) - transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes.
**ZIKA (=Zika virus) - can cause flu-like symptoms and birth defects.

- - Anopheles franciscanus (=An franciscanus)
Related to the mosquito that spreads malaria, avian malaria.
Low concern for now but good species to keep an eye on for the future - locally acquired malaria has been found in Washington State recently.
** Related to the mosquito that spreads malaria, avian malaria.

- - Culex erythrothorax (=Cx erythrothorax)
Vector for West Nile virus, less prevalent in Arizona than other vector species.
Culex quinquefasciatus (=Cx quinquefasciatus)
**WNV (=West Nile virus) - Transmitted by Culex mosquitoes.

- - Culex quinx, affectionately, aka southern house mosquito.
Part of the Cx pipiens complex.
**The main West Nile vector in Arizona due to its prevalence.
